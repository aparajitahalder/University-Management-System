Aesthetic Essence
The Mody University establishes itself to be the best platform where education, recreation and spirituality blend perfectly. The students within the campus get a chance to learn their subjects as well as spiritual values, all at the same time. Besides this, they are also given an opportunity to participate in yoga practices and meditation to relax. Having a peaceful mental state makes it easier for the students to grasp the lessons and other teachings comfortably. Tapovan is the place in Mody University where a blended dais for recreation, spirituality and religion is offered to the girls to help them attain complete serenity.

TAPOVAN
Tapovan is divided into three units, namely: => Temple
=> Yagnashala
=> Meditation hall

YAGYAS
The Temple, the Yagnashala and the Meditation hall constitute the Tapovan area of Mody University. This journey of spirituality began on the 8th day of February in the year 2011 due to the efforts of Mr. Rajendra Prasad Mody, Chairman of Mody University Shaskiya Parishad. The temple is adorned with beautiful idols of Shri Ramchandraji, Mata Sita, Laxman and Hanumanji. This is why it has been named as the “The Ram Darbar”. Along with this, the idol of Surya Dev with his 7 horses is also crafted at the Shikhar, the large dome of the temple. This very work of art indicates the motion-oriented life of the students. For Yagya in the Yagnashala, priests are called from Banaras who recite the Vedas. All the students are called to attend the yagnas that are performed from time to time. The Meditation Hall of Tapovan has a capacity to accommodate 500 people to meditate at a time.
